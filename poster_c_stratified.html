GROUP C: STRATIFIED RANDOM SAMPLE
The Story
A farmer wants to know the average yield of his land, but only has time to measure 5 plots of his total 50. Of the three sampling methods, our group explored STRATIFIED RANDOM SAMPLE.
Our Method

STRATIFIED RANDOM SAMPLE

"We randomly select one plot from each column (strata). Every water level is represented!"

Why stratify? To account for systematic difference in the population. Plots near the river grow more than plots by the road.

Conclusion

What does this mean for Stratified Random Sampling?

BIAS: Stratified sampling is UNBIASED. We don't systematically favor any region of the field.
VARIABILITY: Stratified sampling has LOW VARIATION (High Precision). Even if we pick different rows, we'd still get one from each water level‚Äîso our estimates cluster near the true mean.
VERDICT: Stratified sampling is ACCURATE and CONSISTENT. It's the best choice when you know the population has distinct subgroups!
